# Individual Contribution Reflection

## Technical Contribution

I worked primarily on the exploratory data analysis (EDA), helped merge the PopuList dataset with ESS, and created visualisations showing support patterns across countries. This task proved particularly time-consuming due to the lack of harmonisation between the two datasets. Renaming parties, dropping missing values, selecting key variables, and recoding them correctly were all essential steps that laid the groundwork for our project.

I also conducted preliminary linear regressions and co-created comparative graphs to identify relevant patterns, helping to guide our modelling approach. In addition, I suggested narrowing our supervised learning focus to two countries in the final GBM section, selected those countries, and aligned this approach with our project’s narrative. I helped write the interpretation and motivation sections, ensuring that our supervised learning methodology was justified by our dataset’s structure and project goals.

Throughout the project, I also reviewed teammates’ contributions for clarity, consistency, and reproducibility, helping to ensure a high standard of analysis across the workflow. I additionally conducted further literature review to support our analytical framing and align our work with existing debates on populist voting behaviour.

## Team Collaboration

I had greater availability during the early stages of the project, so I took the lead in exploring and preparing the data for modelling. This included cleaning, integrating, and coding the datasets in a usable format—ensuring that once the exam period began, other team members could work efficiently on modelling without being delayed by upstream issues.

We coordinated regularly via messaging, attended three office hours, and met in person on three occasions to discuss strategy, align deliverables, and delegate tasks effectively. I also helped diffuse potential overlap between members by actively checking progress, asking clarifying questions, and stepping in to support team members when Git conflicts or bugs arose.

## What I Learned

I developed my GitHub skills, particularly around managing branches, staging changes, resolving merge conflicts, and navigating collaborative workflows in a group project. Prior to this, I had limited experience with Git in team environments.

I also improved my ability to write and format `.qmd` documents by learning from more experienced team members. This included using code chunk options for better readability, integrating inline interpretations, and rendering our project in a cohesive and reproducible format.
